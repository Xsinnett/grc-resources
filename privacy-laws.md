# Privacy Laws & Regulations

A breakdown of the most important privacy laws that compliance 
professionals need to understand. Each section covers who the 
law applies to, key requirements, and useful resources.

---

## 1. GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation

**Jurisdiction:** European Union  
**Enacted:** May 25, 2018  
**Enforced by:** Data Protection Authorities (DPAs) in each EU member state  
**Website:** https://gdpr.eu

### Who It Applies To
Any organization that:
- Is established in the EU, or
- Offers goods or services to EU residents, or
- Monitors the behavior of EU residents

### Key Requirements
- Must have a lawful basis for processing personal data
- Must obtain clear consent where consent is the lawful basis
- Must provide a privacy policy in plain language
- Must appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) in certain cases
- Must report data breaches within 72 hours
- Must honor data subject rights requests within 30 days
- Must conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) 
  for high-risk processing

### Individual Rights
| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Right to Access | Request a copy of personal data held |
| Right to Rectification | Request correction of inaccurate data |
| Right to Erasure | Request deletion of personal data |
| Right to Restrict Processing | Limit how data is used |
| Right to Data Portability | Receive data in a portable format |
| Right to Object | Object to processing based on legitimate interests |
| Rights re: Automated Decisions | Not subject to solely automated decisions |

### Penalties
- Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover — whichever 
  is higher — for serious violations
- Up to €10 million or 2% of global annual turnover for 
  less serious violations

### Key Resources
- [Full GDPR Text](https://gdpr-info.eu)
- [GDPR.eu Guide](https://gdpr.eu)
- [EU Data Protection Authorities](https://edpb.europa.eu)

## 2. CCPA/CPRA — California Consumer Privacy Act

**Jurisdiction:** California, United States  
**Enacted:** January 1, 2020 (CCPA) / January 1, 2023 (CPRA amendments)  
**Enforced by:** California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)  
**Website:** https://cppa.ca.gov

### Who It Applies To
For-profit businesses that do business in California AND meet 
at least ONE of the following:
- Annual gross revenue exceeds $25 million
- Buy, sell, or share personal info of 100,000+ consumers 
  or households annually
- Derive 50% or more of annual revenue from selling or sharing 
  personal information

### Key Requirements
- Must provide a privacy policy disclosing data practices
- Must provide a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" 
  link if applicable
- Must respond to consumer rights requests within 45 days
- Must not discriminate against consumers who exercise their rights
- Must have written contracts with all service providers
- Must conduct annual cybersecurity audits (large businesses)

### Individual Rights
| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Right to Know | Know what personal info is collected and how it is used |
| Right to Delete | Request deletion of personal information |
| Right to Correct | Request correction of inaccurate information |
| Right to Opt-Out | Opt out of sale or sharing of personal information |
| Right to Limit | Limit use of sensitive personal information |
| Right to Non-Discrimination | Not be discriminated against for exercising rights |

### Penalties
- Up to $2,500 per unintentional violation
- Up to $7,500 per intentional violation
- Private right of action for data breaches — $100 to $750 
  per consumer per incident

### Key Resources
- [California Privacy Protection Agency](https://cppa.ca.gov)
- [Full CCPA Text](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov)
- [State AG CCPA Resource](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa)

## 3. HIPAA — Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act

**Jurisdiction:** United States  
**Enacted:** 1996 (Privacy Rule effective 2003)  
**Enforced by:** HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR)  
**Website:** https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa

### Who It Applies To
- **Covered Entities** — health plans, health care clearinghouses, 
  and health care providers that transmit health information 
  electronically
- **Business Associates** — persons or entities that perform 
  services for covered entities involving the use or disclosure 
  of protected health information (PHI)

### Key Requirements
- Must implement a HIPAA Privacy Policy
- Must designate a Privacy Officer and Security Officer
- Must conduct regular security risk assessments
- Must implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- Must sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all 
  business associates
- Must notify individuals of breaches within 60 days
- Must train all workforce members on HIPAA requirements
- Must retain documentation for a minimum of 6 years

### The Three Rules
| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Privacy Rule | Standards for the use and disclosure of PHI |
| Security Rule | Safeguards to protect electronic PHI (ePHI) |
| Breach Notification Rule | Requirements for notifying individuals of breaches |

### Penalties
- **Tier 1** — Did not know: $100–$50,000 per violation
- **Tier 2** — Reasonable cause: $1,000–$50,000 per violation
- **Tier 3** — Willful neglect corrected: $10,000–$50,000 per violation
- **Tier 4** — Willful neglect not corrected: $50,000 per violation
- Maximum penalty of $1.9 million per violation category per year
- Criminal penalties up to 10 years imprisonment for serious violations

### Key Resources
- [HHS HIPAA Homepage](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa)
- [HHS Office for Civil Rights](https://www.hhs.gov/ocr)
- [HIPAA Privacy Rule Summary](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy)
- [HIPAA Security Rule Summary](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/security)

## 4. PIPEDA — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act

**Jurisdiction:** Canada  
**Enacted:** 2000 (fully in force 2004)  
**Enforced by:** Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC)  
**Website:** https://www.priv.gc.ca

### Who It Applies To
Private sector organizations that collect, use, or disclose 
personal information in the course of commercial activities 
across Canada, with some exceptions for provinces with 
substantially similar legislation.

### Key Requirements
- Must designate a Privacy Officer accountable for compliance
- Must identify the purpose for collecting personal information 
  before or at time of collection
- Must obtain meaningful consent before collecting personal information
- Must only collect information necessary for identified purposes
- Must protect personal information with appropriate safeguards
- Must make privacy policies readily available
- Must respond to access requests within 30 days
- Must notify individuals and the OPC of qualifying breaches
- Must maintain breach records for 24 months

### The Ten Fair Information Principles
1. Accountability
2. Identifying Purposes
3. Consent
4. Limiting Collection
5. Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention
6. Accuracy
7. Safeguards
8. Openness
9. Individual Access
10. Challenging Compliance

### Penalties
- Up to $100,000 CAD for knowingly committing certain violations
- Organizations found non-compliant may be taken to Federal Court

### Key Resources
- [Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.priv.gc.ca)
- [Full PIPEDA Text](https://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca)
- [PIPEDA Fair Information Principles](https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/p_principle)

## 5. Other Notable Privacy Laws

### FERPA — Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act
- **Jurisdiction:** United States
- **Applies to:** Educational institutions that receive federal funding
- **Protects:** Student education records
- **Website:** https://studentprivacy.ed.gov

### COPPA — Children's Online Privacy Protection Act
- **Jurisdiction:** United States
- **Applies to:** Websites and online services directed at children 
  under 13
- **Protects:** Personal information of children under 13
- **Website:** https://www.ftc.gov/coppa

### LGPD — Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados
- **Jurisdiction:** Brazil
- **Enacted:** September 18, 2020
- **Similar to:** GDPR in structure and requirements
- **Website:** https://www.gov.br/lgpd

### POPIA — Protection of Personal Information Act
- **Jurisdiction:** South Africa
- **Enacted:** July 1, 2021
- **Similar to:** GDPR in structure and requirements
- **Website:** https://www.justice.gov.za/inforeg
